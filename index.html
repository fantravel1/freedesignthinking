Our Mission

We believe that design thinking should be accessible to everyone, especially young Black and Brown students who have been historically underrepresented in design and innovation spaces. Our mission is to create the world's most comprehensive free design thinking education platform.

Through curated video content from leading institutions like Stanford, Harvard, and MIT, combined with access to global design thinking communities, we're building a network of diverse innovators ready to tackle the world's most pressing challenges.

In the near future, we will feature 100% Black and Brown design thinking instructors and content creators, ensuring that representation matters at every level of education and inspiration.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is design thinking?

Design thinking is a non-linear, iterative process that teams use to understand users, challenge assumptions, redefine problems and create innovative solutions. It includes phases like empathy, ideation, prototyping, and testing to solve complex problems in a human-centered way. Learn more ‚Üí

Is this free design thinking course really free?

Yes, absolutely! Our free design thinking course includes all 250+ videos and community resources at no cost. We believe design thinking education should be accessible to everyone, especially Black and Brown students globally. No hidden fees, no premium tiers.

What design thinking resources are available?

Our design thinking resources include: 250+ curated videos from Stanford d.school, Harvard, and MIT; printable worksheets and templates; brainstorming technique guides; prototyping tutorials; creative warm-up exercises; and guided learning pathways called Design Menus. Explore all resources ‚Üí

What is brainstorming in design thinking?

Brainstorming in design thinking is the ideation phase where teams generate as many ideas as possible without judgment. Key principles include: defer judgment, encourage wild ideas, build on others' ideas, and go for quantity. Learn brainstorming techniques ‚Üí

What are creative warm-up exercises?

Creative warm-up exercises are quick activities done before design thinking sessions to activate imagination and build team energy. Examples include the 30 circles challenge, yes-and improv games, and word association games. Browse 70+ warm-up exercises ‚Üí

Who can use this platform?

Anyone can use our platform! While we specifically designed it for Black and Brown students globally, all learners are welcome to access our free design thinking resources. Students, educators, professionals, and anyone interested in creative problem-solving can benefit.

Do I need any prior experience?

No prior experience required! Our content ranges from beginner to advanced levels. Start with warm-up exercises and progress through brainstorming, prototyping, testing, and reflection at your own pace. Each video includes difficulty ratings to help you choose the right level.

How do I get started?

Simply browse our video library above and start watching! We recommend beginning with a warm-up exercise to get your creative energy flowing, then moving to brainstorming techniques. Each phase of the design thinking process builds on the previous one.

Can I use these videos for teaching?

Yes! Educators are encouraged to use our curated video library in their classrooms and workshops. All content is from reputable sources including Stanford, Harvard, and MIT, making it perfect for educational purposes.
