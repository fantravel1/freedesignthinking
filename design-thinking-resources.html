What Are Design Thinking Resources?

Design thinking resources are educational materials, tools, and guides that help individuals and teams learn, practice, and apply the design thinking methodology. These resources range from introductory videos explaining core concepts to advanced templates for facilitating workshops.

Quality design thinking resources share several characteristics: they're practical and actionable, they come from reputable sources, they cover all phases of the design thinking process, and they're accessible to learners at different skill levels.

Types of Design Thinking Resources

Video Tutorials

Video content is one of the most effective ways to learn design thinking because it shows techniques in action. Our library includes videos from Stanford d.school demonstrating facilitation techniques, Harvard i-lab sessions on innovation, and IDEO case studies showing real-world applications.

Worksheets and Templates

Practical templates help structure your design thinking sessions. Essential templates include:

  • Empathy Maps: Capture what users say, think, feel, and do
  • Journey Maps: Visualize the user experience over time
  • Brainstorming Canvases: Structure ideation sessions
  • Prototype Planning Sheets: Plan what to build and test
  • Feedback Capture Grids: Organize user testing insights
