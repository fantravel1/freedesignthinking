Timeline: The Evolution of Design Thinking

Design thinking evolved over several decades, with each era adding new dimensions:

Horst Rittel: "Wicked Problems"

Design theorist Horst Rittel introduces the concept of "wicked problems"â€”complex challenges that resist traditional problem-solving approaches.

Architect Peter Rowe publishes "Design Thinking," one of the first uses of the term, analyzing how architects and planners approach problems.

Tim Brown's book becomes the definitive guide to design thinking for business leaders, selling hundreds of thousands of copies globally.

2010s

Global Adoption

2020s

Evolution Continues

Design thinking integrates with AI, systems thinking, and sustainability. The methodology evolves while core principles remain relevant.

What is IDEO?

IDEO is a global design and innovation company founded in 1991. It's considered the birthplace of modern design thinking methodology.

IDEO was formed when David Kelley Design, ID Two (founded by Bill Moggridge, designer of the first laptop), and Matrix Product Design merged. The company became famous for creating innovative products, including:

  • The first Apple mouse - Setting the standard for computer interaction
  • Palm V PDA - Iconic handheld device design
  • Steelcase Leap chair - Revolutionary ergonomic office seating
  • Shimano coasting bikes - Redesigning cycling for casual riders

Beyond products, IDEO pioneered human-centered design methods that became the foundation of design thinking. The company's "deep dive" processâ€”rapid prototyping, user research, and cross-disciplinary collaborationâ€”influenced organizations worldwide.

What is Stanford d.school?

The Hasso Plattner Institute of Design at Stanford (d.school) was founded in 2005 by David Kelley with a $35 million donation from SAP co-founder Hasso Plattner.

The d.school's mission is to make design thinking accessible to students from all disciplinesâ€”not just designers. It developed the now-famous 5-stage process:

  1. Empathize - Understand users through research
  2. Define - Articulate the problem clearly
  3. Ideate - Generate creative solutions
  4. Prototype - Build to think
  5. Test - Learn from feedback

The d.school's influence extends far beyond Stanford. Its curriculum, bootcamp format, and teaching methods have been adopted by universities and organizations worldwide. Many d.school alumni have founded companies and programs that spread design thinking globally.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the father of design thinking?

+
Herbert Simon is often called the "father of design thinking" for establishing its intellectual foundations. However, David Kelley is considered the father of modern, practical design thinking for developing the methodology at IDEO and founding Stanford's d.school. Both made essential contributions.

When was design thinking invented?

+
Design thinking evolved over decades. The conceptual foundation was laid in 1969 by Herbert Simon. The term was first used by Peter Rowe in 1987. The practical methodology was developed by IDEO in the 1990s. The methodology as we know it today crystallized in the mid-2000s with Stanford d.school.

Did Stanford invent design thinking?

+
Stanford didn't invent design thinking, but the d.school made it accessible and teachable. The practical methodology was developed by IDEO (co-founded by Stanford professor David Kelley). The d.school, founded in 2005, created structured curriculum and the famous 5-stage process that spread design thinking globally.

What did IDEO contribute to design thinking?

+
IDEO developed the practical methodology for design thinking: rapid prototyping, user research techniques, cross-disciplinary collaboration, and the "fail fast" philosophy. IDEO's work with companies like Apple, Procter & Gamble, and healthcare organizations demonstrated design thinking's real-world impact.

Is design thinking the same as human-centered design?

+
Design thinking and human-centered design (HCD) are closely related but not identical. Human-centered design is the broader philosophy of putting humans at the center of the design process. Design thinking is a specific methodology that applies HCD principles through structured stages. IDEO uses both terms, with design thinking being more associated with business applications.
